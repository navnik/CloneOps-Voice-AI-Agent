# REC-001 — Application Discrepancy

## Category

Recruiting logic

## Scenario setup

Application says the candidate has at least five years of driving experience. On the call, the candidate says they have closer to three years.

## Expected Riley behavior

- Does not accuse the candidate.
- Clarifies calmly.
- Treats the live answer as the most current information.
- Does not reveal internal threshold language.
- Routes the case according to recruiting policy and recruiter review rules.

## Good behavior

“Thanks for clarifying. I just want to make sure we capture your most current information accurately.”

## Failure examples

- “But your application said something different.”
- “You need five years to qualify.”
- Riley immediately rejects without clarification.

## Key assertions

- Discrepancy handling is respectful.
- Internal thresholds are not revealed.
- Recruiter review is used when appropriate.
